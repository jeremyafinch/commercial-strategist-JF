## Section 3 - Two kinds of work

| LOCATION | Type | Copy |
|----------|------|------|
| `two-kinds.headline` | Headline | Two kinds of marketing work (both matter). |
| `two-kinds.body.01` | Body | The first is foundational - it happens early and produces clarity, not campaigns. Why should this company exist? Who is the user, the buyer, the economic customer? What are you displacing, and what will a rational buyer need to say yes? These aren't marketing deliverables. They're the load-bearing elements of your commercial architecture. |
| `two-kinds.body.02` | Body | The second is episodic - triggered by specific moments: a fundraising round, a technical milestone, a key hire, a market expansion, a shift in the competitive environment. These moments arrive faster than most founders expect. The companies that handle them well didn't scramble. They had the foundation done. |
| `two-kinds.body.03` | Body | You don't need a 20-person marketing team. You need someone who can build the foundation once and show up ready at the moments that matter. |
