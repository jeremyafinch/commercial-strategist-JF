Point of view

Marketing isn't just the last mile.

If you wait until it's ready, you're already too late.

The worst time to figure out your market story is when you urgently need it. Yet many technical founders think of marketing as something you bolt on at the end, when the product is finally ready. In hard tech, that instinct will cost you time and runway.

When development cycles last a decade and capital requirements run into the tens of millions, your market story isn't a launch asset. It's a design input - one that shapes what you build, who you hire, and whether money shows up in time.

Crafting the narratives

Adjacent industries, regulators, acquirers - each needs a variation on the story.

Two kinds of marketing work (both matter).

The first is foundational - it happens early and produces clarity, not campaigns. Why should this company exist? Who is the user, the buyer, the economic customer? What are you displacing, and what will a rational buyer need to say yes? These aren't marketing deliverables. They're the load-bearing elements of your commercial architecture.

The second is episodic - triggered by specific moments: a fundraising round, a technical milestone, a key hire, a market expansion, a shift in the competitive environment. These moments arrive faster than most founders expect. The companies that handle them well didn't scramble. They had the foundation done.

You don't need a 20-person marketing team. You need someone who can build the foundation once and show up ready at the moments that matter.

Hard tech - Materials science

Lux Labs

Company

Lux Labs built transparent display materials and projection film - an MIT spinout turning advanced materials science into a new consumer and commercial product.

Tension

Real technology, early customers, but a go-to-market architecture that wasn't built for scale - 30 SKUs, low sales, unclear positioning, margins under pressure.

What I did

Simplified the catalog from 30 SKUs to two core offerings, rebuilt pricing model and persona-based e-commerce website, aligned messaging to drive conversion.

Outcome

100% ROI in six weeks through improved gross margins. That pricing and packaging structure stayed intact through acquisition - the ultimate stress test for whether the commercial architecture could actually hold.

Hard tech - Construction robotics

Reframe Systems

Company

Reframe Systems is building robotics and automation technology for prefab construction - a technically complex solution for an industry resistant to change.

Tension

The founders are brilliant and the technology was real. But the story was hard to convey, and the company was struggling to balance the outcomes-based story of what they do with the technology story of how they did it.

What I did

Rather than write the story for them, I coached one of the cofounders to tell it himself - on camera, in their own words. The goal was helping the co-founder tell his story authentically in his own way, not just sound like a pitch deck.

Outcome

What came out was something no polished copy could replicate. The video became a recruiting asset, a partner conversation opener, and a PR and GTM piece simultaneously. Sometimes the right format changes everything.
